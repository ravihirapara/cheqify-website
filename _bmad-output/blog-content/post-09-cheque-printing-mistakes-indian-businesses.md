## Body (English)

Every bounced cheque costs your business — not just the bank penalty of ₹150-750, but also damaged supplier relationships and delayed payments. Most bounces are caused by avoidable printing errors. Here are the top 5 mistakes and how to prevent them.

### [H2] 1. Wrong date format

Different banks accept different date formats. Some require DD/MM/YYYY, others accept DD-MM-YYYY. Writing "4/13/2026" (American format) on an Indian cheque will get it rejected instantly.

The fix: Cheqify auto-formats the date according to each bank's specific requirements. You just pick the date, and the format is handled automatically.

### [H2] 2. Amount in words doesn't match figures

"Twenty-five thousand" written in words but "2,500" in figures is the most common cause of cheque rejection. Manual conversion is error-prone, especially for large amounts with paise.

The fix: Cheqify's smart auto-fill converts the numeric amount to words instantly — including paise — eliminating any mismatch.

### [H2] 3. Printing outside the designated area

Every bank's cheque has different field positions. Using a generic template or aligning by eye often results in text printing over the MICR code band or outside field boundaries. Banks reject these cheques outright.

The fix: Cheqify has 300+ pre-built bank layouts with exact field positions. Select your bank, and every field prints precisely where it should.

### [H2] 4. Missing or incorrect payee name

Spelling "Reliance Industries Limited" as "Reliance Ind. Ltd" can cause a cheque to be returned. Banks match payee names strictly, and abbreviations or typos trigger rejections.

The fix: Cheqify's saved contacts feature lets you store exact payee names and reuse them. Type once, use everywhere — no more spelling variations across cheques.

### [H2] 5. Not tracking cheque numbers

Accidentally reusing a cheque number or skipping numbers creates accounting chaos. Without a tracking system, you only discover the problem when the bank flags it — often weeks later.

The fix: Cheqify's chequebook management tracks every cheque number across all your bank accounts. It auto-assigns the next available number and alerts you about gaps or duplicates.

### [H2] The bottom line

These 5 mistakes account for over 80% of cheque rejections in Indian businesses. The common thread? They're all caused by manual processes. Switching to Cheqify eliminates every single one of them — and it's completely free.
