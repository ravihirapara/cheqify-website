## The Problem with Manual Cheque Writing

Despite the growth of digital payments, cheques remain essential for many Indian businesses — from vendor payments and rent to government transactions and security deposits. But writing cheques by hand comes with real problems.

### Common Issues with Handwritten Cheques

- **Illegible handwriting** — Poor handwriting is the leading cause of cheque rejection at banks.
- **Amount mismatch** — Writing the amount in both figures and words by hand often leads to discrepancies.
- **Overwriting and corrections** — A single mistake means starting over with a new cheque leaf, wasting time and resources.
- **Slow process** — Writing 10-20 cheques for monthly vendor payments can take over an hour.
- **No tracking** — Once a handwritten cheque is issued, you have no visibility into whether it was deposited or cleared.
- **No audit trail** — There is no automatic record of who wrote the cheque, when, or what changes were made.

For businesses issuing even 5-10 cheques per month, these problems add up to significant wasted time and increased risk.
